Stateside Traveler

Main duties of my application

  • Create a profile

  • Keep track of the States you have been to

  • What did you do when you were there

  • How would you rate your visit

  • What is there to do in states that you haven't been to

  • Suggestions based on what you have done in the past

Create a profile

The first thing that you will need to do is make a profile for yourself. This will allow you to put in personal information such as your name and your email. It will be kept private. You will also have the option to upload a photo of yourself.

Keep track of the states you have been to

Once your profile has been created, now you will have the opportunity to enter in all the States that you have been to and the places that you saw. The program will be able to differentiate where you have and have not been.

What did you do when you were there

After you have entered the places that you have been to, you can now go into detail on the things that you did. Once you have done that, you can categorize it based on the type of activity. You will be presented with the categories so that you can group them accordingly.

How would you rate your visit

You will be presented with a scale to determine how the visit went. It will be a scale of 1 to 10, with 10 being fantastic and 1 being horrible. There are a few factors to consider in your rating. You will provide a rating for the food, the people in the area, what things there were to do, and those will be averaged together to give you your overall rating.

What is there to do in states that you haven't been to

The program will keep track of the states that you have not visited yet. Should you decide to see where you need to go, you will have the option to view those states.

Suggestions on what to do in those states

For the states that you have not visited yet, you will have the option to explore possible activities that the state is known for. That will include the top ten restaurants, the top ten in activities, and the ten in places to see such as parks or museums.
